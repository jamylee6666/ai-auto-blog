## Opening: Why Mock Interviews Matter (And Why You're Probably Doing Them Wrong)

You've got an interview scheduled in two weeks. You're nervous. You've Googled "common interview questions," read 47 blog posts, and maybe practiced once with a friend who gave you vague feedback like "you sounded good?" 

Here's the problem: without targeted, repeated practice against someone (or something) that actually challenges you, you'll walk into that real interview underprepared. You won't know which answers are too long. You won't catch that nervous habit of saying "um" every third sentence. You won't practice the pause-and-think technique that makes you sound thoughtful instead of panicked.

This is where an **AI interview coach** changes the game. Instead of waiting for a friend's availability, you can run unlimited **AI mock interviews** whenever you want—at 6 AM, 11 PM, during your lunch break. The AI gives you instant feedback, helps you refine answers for *your specific job* and *your specific company*, and tracks your improvements over time.

By the end of this guide, you'll have a personalized interview preparation system that works 24/7, costs almost nothing, and eliminates the guesswork from career development.

## Step-by-Step Guide: Build Your AI Interview Prep System

### Step 1: Create a Detailed Interview Brief

Before you practice, you need context. The more specific your setup, the better your feedback.

**What to do:**
1. Open a Google Doc or Notion page titled "Interview Prep – [Company Name]"
2. Fill in these sections:
   - **Job title** (e.g., "Senior Marketing Manager")
   - **Company name and industry** (e.g., "TechStartup Inc., B2B SaaS")
   - **Key responsibilities** from the job posting (copy-paste 3–4 bullet points)
   - **Company values or mission** (check their website or LinkedIn)
   - **Your relevant experience** (2–3 bullet points about your background)
   - **Gaps you're worried about** (e.g., "No direct SaaS experience" or "Haven't led a team")

**Example:**
```
Job Title: Content Marketing Manager
Company: GrowthLabs (marketing automation platform)
Key Responsibility: Drive content strategy for product-led growth
Company Mission: Democratize marketing automation for SMBs
My Background: 5 years in content marketing, mostly B2C
Worried About: Haven't done product-focused content before
```

Save this—you'll reference it in every mock session. This specificity is what makes AI coaching effective instead of generic.

### Step 2: Set Up Your AI Interview Coach

Now you'll use ChatGPT or Claude to become your interviewer.

**Using ChatGPT Plus:**
1. Open ChatGPT and start a new chat
2. Paste this prompt:

```
You are an expert job interview coach and interviewer for [COMPANY NAME]. 
You're conducting a mock interview for the role of [JOB TITLE].

Key context:
- Company mission: [PASTE MISSION]
- Main responsibility: [PASTE JOB DESCRIPTION]
- Candidate background: [PASTE YOUR EXPERIENCE]

Your job:
1. Ask tough, role-specific interview questions (not generic ones)
2. Challenge vague answers and ask for specific examples
3. After 5–6 questions, give written feedback on:
   - Clarity (was the answer easy to follow?)
   - Relevance (did it address the job?)
   - Confidence (tone, pacing, filler words?)
   - Specific improvements

Start with your first question now.
```

3. Hit enter and let the AI ask your first question

**Using Claude (Free):**
Same prompt works—just paste it into Claude's chat interface at Claude.ai.

The AI will now ask you interview questions tailored to the specific role and company, not generic ones. This matters because you're not just preparing for "any job"—you're preparing for *this* job.

### Step 3: Answer and Record (Without Perfection)

Here's where many people mess up: they type their answers. Typing feels easy, but interviews are *spoken*. You need to practice out loud.

**What to do:**
1. Read the AI's question aloud (or in your head—your choice)
2. **Speak your answer out loud.** No reading from notes. Act like you're in the real interview.
3. Type what you said (or paste a summary) into the chat
4. Let the AI respond

**Pro tip:** If you want video feedback, hit Record on Google Meet or Zoom *before* you start. Play it back later and note:
- How many times did you say "um," "uh," or "like"?
- Did you pause before answering (a sign of thoughtfulness)?
- Was your voice confident or hesitant?

### Step 4: Get Feedback and Refine

After each question, the AI will give you feedback. But don't just read it and move on—act on it.

**What to do:**
1. Read the AI's feedback on your answer
2. Copy your answer into your Google Doc with the feedback below it
3. Ask the AI: *"Can you give me a better version of that answer that addresses [specific feedback]? Keep it under 60 seconds."*
4. The AI rewrites it; you practice saying the rewritten version out loud
5. Save both versions in your document

**Example interaction:**
- **Your answer:** "I increased our social media followers by 40% over six months."
- **AI feedback:** "Good metric, but no detail. What strategy? What channels? Why did it work?"
- **You ask:** "Rewrite that answer to show my specific strategy, in under 60 seconds."
- **AI:** "Over six months, I focused our budget on LinkedIn and TikTok, targeting mid-market SaaS buyers. I created a series of educational carousel posts showing how-tos and industry insights. By being consistent 3x per week and engaging in comments daily, we saw 40% growth, but more importantly, 12% of new followers were qualified leads who eventually converted to demo requests."

Save the *refined* version and practice it 2–3 times.

### Step 5: Run a Full Mock Interview Session (5–6 Questions)

Once you've practiced a few individual questions, run an entire mock interview in one session.

**What to do:**
1. Reset your chat (or start a new one with the same prompt)
2. Tell the AI: *"Let's do a full mock interview. Ask me 5–6 questions in sequence, and don't tell me what's coming next. After the sixth question, give me overall feedback."*
3. Answer each question without stopping to review or revise—treat it like the real thing
4. Take notes after each answer about what felt shaky
5. Get the AI's overall feedback

This full-session practice is crucial because interviews are endurance tests. You need to know you can stay sharp across multiple questions, not just nail one at a time.

### Step 6: Track Your Progress

Create a simple scorecard to see improvements over time.

**What to do:**
1. In Google Docs, create a table:

| Session | Date | Questions Asked | Clarity Score (1–10) | Confidence Score (1–10) | Key Feedback | Improvements Made |
|---------|------|-----------------|----------------------|-------------------------|--------------|-------------------|
| Session 1 | Jan 15 | "Tell me about yourself" | 6 | 5 | Rambled; used filler words | Tighten to 30 seconds |

2. After each mock interview, fill in a row
3. Watch your scores climb as you practice—this visual progress is motivating and shows where to focus

### Step 7: Tackle Your Weaknesses with Targeted Drills

Once you've identified weak areas (common answers: vagueness, nervousness, rambling), run focused practice sessions.

**What to do:**
1. Ask the AI: *"I ramble when I'm nervous. Give me 3 tough questions about my experience where I'm likely to over-explain. Ask them in sequence."*
2. For each answer, push yourself to be concise—aim for 45–60 seconds
3. The AI will tell you if you're still over-explaining
4. Repeat until you hit your target

This targeted approach beats general practice. You're not trying to get better at everything—you're systematically crushing your specific weaknesses.

## Advanced Tips: Go Deeper

### Tip 1: Record and Review Video

After your final 1–2 mock sessions, record yourself on video (Zoom, Google Meet, or even your phone). Watch it back as if you're the interviewer. You'll catch nervous habits you didn't know you had:
- Fidgeting with your hands
- Not making "eye contact" with the camera
- Speaking too fast under pressure
- Weak posture

Ask the AI: *"Based on my video, what three things should I work on before the real interview?"* 

### Tip 2: Prepare a "Story Bank"

Interviewers love specific examples. Create a Google Doc with 8–10 stories from your career:
- A time you failed and recovered
- A time you led a team through a difficult project
- A time you improved a process or metric
- A time you handled a difficult colleague

For each story, write it out using the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result). During your mock interviews, ask the AI to probe these stories with follow-up questions. This makes your real answers concrete and memorable.

### Tip 3: Research Like an AI Researcher

Before your final mock session, ask the AI:
*"I'm interviewing at [COMPANY NAME] for [JOB TITLE]. What 5 things should I know about this company's recent moves, products, or culture? Give me insider-level insights I can reference in an interview to show I've done my homework."*

The AI synthesizes public information into conversation-ready insights. You can then weave these into your answers: *"I noticed you recently launched [product]—how does this role support that strategy?"*

This separates you from the 90% of candidates who only did surface-level research.
