## The Content Goldmine You're Ignoring (And How to Mine It)

You've written a blog post. Maybe it took three hours. It got decent traffic, a few comments, and then... nothing. It sits in your archive while you scramble to write the next one.

Here's the hard truth: **one blog post can become five pieces of content—social media clips, email newsletters, infographics, podcast scripts, LinkedIn articles—without writing anything from scratch.** The problem is knowing *how* to transform it without sounding like you're just republishing the same thing.

That's where **Claude AI and strategic repurposing** come in. Instead of starting from a blank page every time, you'll use Claude to intelligently break down your existing content into different formats, angles, and platforms. This isn't just copy-paste recycling—it's smart content multiplication that saves 10-15 hours of writing per week.

By the end of this guide, you'll know exactly how to turn one blog post into a full month of content across multiple channels. Let's start.

## Step-by-Step Guide: Repurposing Your Blog Post with Claude

### Step 1: Copy Your Blog Post and Paste It into Claude

Open Claude and create a new chat. Paste your entire blog post into the conversation. Keep the formatting clean—just copy the text without extra HTML or markdown if possible.

Include a quick header so Claude knows what you're working with:

```
Here's my blog post about [topic]:

[paste full blog post]
```

For example: "Here's my blog post about automating email management with AI tools."

Claude will instantly absorb the content and be ready to help you break it down.

### Step 2: Ask Claude to Create a Social Media Content Plan

This is where the magic happens. Send Claude this prompt:

```
Create 5 short-form social media posts (150-280 characters each) from this blog post. 
Make each one punchy and shareable for Twitter/X, LinkedIn, and Instagram. 
Include a relevant emoji for each. Format as a numbered list.
```

Claude will generate a list like:

1. "🧠 One blog post. Five pieces of content. Stop writing from scratch—learn how AI can repurpose your best work in minutes. [Link]"
2. "📱 Your old blog posts are sitting idle. Claude AI can transform one article into: social posts, email sequences, infographics, podcasts + more."

**What you get:** 5 ready-to-use social posts you can schedule across platforms immediately. Copy these directly into your social media scheduler (Buffer, Later, etc.).

### Step 3: Extract Key Takeaways for an Email Newsletter

Use this prompt:

```
Extract the 3-5 most important takeaways from this blog post. 
Format them as bullet points that would work in a short email to subscribers (200 words max).
Make it conversational, not robotic. Include one call-to-action at the end.
```

Claude will generate something like:

**Email body:**
"Hey [Name],

Here's what I learned this week:

• Your old content is worth more than you think—one post can become 5 pieces
• AI handles the heavy lifting: formatting, tone-shifting, repurposing
• Start with social snippets, then move to bigger formats (whitepapers, guides)

**Ready to try it?** Forward this email to yourself—we'll walk through it step-by-step next week.

—[Your name]"

**What you get:** A ready-to-send newsletter that promotes your original post to people who might have missed it.

### Step 4: Turn Your Post into a LinkedIn Article (Expanded Version)

LinkedIn articles perform differently than blog posts—they need a different hook and pacing. Send this prompt:

```
Rewrite this blog post as a LinkedIn article (800-1200 words). 
Use a personal, first-person hook at the start about why this topic matters to professionals.
Break it into shorter paragraphs than the original. 
Add "Key Takeaway" call-outs in bold.
Make it feel like professional advice from a peer, not a website article.
```

Claude will restructure your post with a more personal angle, shorter paragraphs, and strategic formatting that works on LinkedIn.

**What you get:** A cross-platform version that can reach your LinkedIn network without cannibalizing your original post's SEO.

### Step 5: Create an Infographic Brief (For a Designer or DIY Tool)

Use this prompt:

```
Create a visual infographic brief based on this blog post.
Include:
- A catchy headline
- 5-7 key statistics or data points to visualize
- Icons/symbols that represent each point
- Color suggestions (modern, professional)
Format it like a creative brief a designer could use.
```

Claude will give you a structured brief:

**Infographic: "How AI Repurposing Saves 15 Hours Per Week"**

**Visual 1:** One blog icon → Five content format icons (time saved: 15 hrs)
**Visual 2:** Clock showing time breakdown (3 hrs to write → 30 mins to repurpose)
**Visual 3:** Platform icons with reach numbers...

**What you get:** A designer-ready brief that cuts your design time in half. If you use Canva Pro, you can even ask Claude to suggest layouts and Canva-compatible templates.

### Step 6: Extract a Podcast/Video Script Outline

Podcasters and video creators need snappy scripts. Use this prompt:

```
Create a 5-7 minute podcast episode outline from this blog post.
Include:
- An opening hook (30 seconds)
- 3 main talking points with timestamps
- A closing call-to-action
Format it as a script outline with speaker notes.
```

Claude generates:

**[0:00-0:30] HOOK:**
"If you're spending 20 hours writing content every month, I have bad news—you're doing it wrong. Today, I'm showing you how I cut that in half using AI."

**[0:30-2:15] Point 1: The Problem**
"Most content creators treat each piece as a one-off..."

**What you get:** A structured outline you can record directly from, or hand to a video editor.

### Step 7: Create a FAQ/Frequently Asked Questions Page

Final repurposing angle. Send this prompt:

```
Based on this blog post, generate 8-10 FAQ-style Q&A pairs that someone learning this topic might ask.
Format as:
Q: [Question]
A: [Short answer, 2-3 sentences, in simple language]

Make them practical and beginner-friendly.
```

Claude generates:

**Q: Can I repurpose posts I wrote 2 years ago?**
A: Yes. Claude doesn't care about publication date. If the information is still relevant, it's worth repurposing. Update any outdated stats and you're good.

**Q: Do I need to mention the original post in repurposed content?**
A: On social posts, no. On longer formats like LinkedIn articles or whitepapers, link back to the original for SEO credit.

**What you get:** A FAQ page that ranks for long-tail keywords and gives Claude AI an SEO boost across your site.

## Advanced Tips: Level Up Your Repurposing Game

**Tip 1: Create a Repurposing Template**
After you've repurposed 2-3 posts, you'll notice patterns. Save Claude prompts you like in a Notion database or Google Doc labeled "Content Repurposing Prompts." Include:
- Prompts that worked best
- Formatting notes for each platform
- A simple checklist (edit? add links? proof?)

Next time, you just copy the exact prompt and paste your new post—Claude handles the rest.

**Tip 2: A/B Test Repurposed Content**
Track which repurposed format performs best:
- Did the social posts get more engagement than the email?
- Did the LinkedIn article drive more blog traffic?

After 3-4 months, double down on your winning format. If LinkedIn articles drive 40% of your traffic but podcasts drive 5%, spend more time on LinkedIn repurposing.

**Tip 3: Combine with Automation**
Pair this with a tool like Zapier or native scheduling features to auto-publish repurposed social content. Some workflows:
- Save repurposed social posts → Buffer → auto-schedule across platforms
- Publish email newsletter → trigger LinkedIn article publication 3 days later
- Extract FAQ → create Notion database that auto-updates your website

This turns repurposing from a one-time task into a repeatable system.
