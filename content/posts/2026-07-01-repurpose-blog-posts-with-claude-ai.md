## Why Your Old Blog Posts Are Wasting Your Potential

You've spent months (or years) writing blog posts that rank well, drive traffic, and bring in readers. But after a post goes live and ages a few months, it sits there—static, forgotten, doing only what it was originally designed to do.

Here's the reality: that 2,000-word post you wrote about "Email Management Best Practices" could become:
- A LinkedIn carousel series
- A YouTube script
- An email course
- A podcast episode outline
- An infographic
- A Twitter thread
- A downloadable checklist
- A client case study

The pain point is clear: content repurposing takes *time*. You'd need to manually rewrite each piece, adapt the tone, restructure it for different platforms, and ensure it still makes sense. Most freelancers and office workers don't have 8 hours to hand-craft five variations of one blog post.

That's where Claude AI comes in. Claude is exceptional at understanding context, rewriting for tone and format, and maintaining consistency across multiple versions of content. In this guide, you'll learn exactly how to take an old blog post and generate three to five repurposed versions—in under 30 minutes.

## Step-by-Step Guide: Repurpose a Blog Post with Claude AI

### Step 1: Copy Your Original Blog Post into Claude

First, log in to [Claude.ai](https://claude.ai) (or open your Claude API if you use the paid tier). Start a new conversation.

Copy the entire blog post—headline, body, any subheadings—and paste it into Claude's chat window. Include a brief context note:

```
I've written a blog post on email management. Here's the full post:

[PASTE YOUR BLOG POST HERE]

I want to repurpose this into multiple formats. Let's start with a LinkedIn carousel and a YouTube video script outline.
```

Paste the full post. Claude's large context window (200K tokens in 2026) means there's no penalty for posting a 2,000-word article.

**Why this works**: Claude sees the entire piece at once, so it understands the flow, key points, and overall message—leading to more coherent repurposed versions.

### Step 2: Generate a LinkedIn Carousel Version

In the same Claude conversation, ask:

```
Now, convert this blog post into a LinkedIn carousel (5–7 slides). 

Each slide should:
- Stand alone but connect to the next
- Be scannable (short sentences, bullet points where helpful)
- Include a clear takeaway or actionable tip
- Use casual-professional tone

Format the output with slide numbers (Slide 1:, Slide 2:, etc.).
```

Claude will generate a carousel script optimized for LinkedIn's algorithm—short, punchy, and designed to keep readers scrolling.

**Pro tip**: Copy this output into Notion or Google Docs immediately. You'll collect all repurposed versions in one place.

### Step 3: Create a YouTube Video Script Outline

Still in the same conversation:

```
Now turn this post into a YouTube video script outline (8–12 minutes long). 

Include:
- Hook (30 seconds) to grab attention
- 3–4 main sections (each ~2 minutes)
- Actionable tips viewers can implement
- Call-to-action at the end
- Conversational tone, not robotic

Format as: [TIMING] Section Title: content here
```

Claude will structure your blog post as a video narrative—with pacing cues and audience engagement hooks. This is radically different from the written format, and Claude handles the translation smoothly.

### Step 4: Generate an Email Course Outline

Ask Claude:

```
Create an email course outline from this blog post. 

The course should have:
- 5 emails (one per day)
- Each email ~300 words
- Progressive learning (Email 1 introduces the problem, Email 5 provides advanced tips)
- A compelling subject line for each email
- A CTA (call-to-action) in each email

Format as: Email 1 - Subject: [subject] | Body: [content]
```

Claude will break down your blog post into a digestible email sequence that keeps readers engaged over five days. This is *gold* for building an email list and nurturing leads.

### Step 5: Create a Twitter/X Thread

In the same conversation:

```
Turn this blog post into a Twitter thread (10–12 tweets). 

Each tweet should:
- Be under 280 characters
- Connect to the next logically
- Include ONE key takeaway or stat
- Use simple, direct language
- Include a hook at the start to make people want to read the full thread

Format as: Tweet 1: [content] | Tweet 2: [content], etc.
```

This is perfect for driving traffic back to your original post and establishing authority in your niche on social media.

### Step 6: Ask for a Downloadable Checklist

One more repurposed format:

```
Create a downloadable checklist based on this blog post. 

The checklist should:
- Be actionable (readers can tick off items)
- Have 12–15 items
- Be organized into 3–4 sections
- Include a brief intro paragraph
- Be formatted for PDF (simple, clean layout)

This will be a lead magnet on my website.
```

Checklists are high-converting lead magnets. Claude will extract the actionable steps from your blog and reformat them as a checklist people actually want to download.

### Step 7: Review, Edit, and Collect

Now you have:
- 1 LinkedIn carousel
- 1 YouTube script outline
- 1 email course (5 emails)
- 1 Twitter thread
- 1 downloadable checklist

Copy all outputs into a single Google Doc or Notion page for final review. Spend 5–10 minutes editing for:
- Brand voice consistency
- Factual accuracy
- Tone match across platforms
- Any jarring phrases Claude may have inserted

This is your *content repurposing asset pack* from a single blog post.

## Advanced Tips: Level Up Your Repurposing Workflow

### Tip 1: Create a Repurposing Template in Claude

Instead of giving Claude instructions from scratch each time, save a "master prompt" in a Notes app or Notion:

```
You are a content repurposing expert. I'll give you a blog post. Repurpose it into:
1. LinkedIn carousel (5–7 slides, conversational tone)
2. YouTube script outline (8–12 minutes, with [TIMING] cues)
3. Email course (5 emails, progressive learning)
4. Twitter thread (10–12 tweets, under 280 chars each)
5. Downloadable checklist (12–15 items, organized into 3–4 sections)

Maintain consistent brand voice across all formats. Format each section clearly with headers.
```

Paste this template + your blog post into Claude once, and you'll get all five formats in one go—cutting your time down to 15–20 minutes.

### Tip 2: Batch Repurpose Old Posts

Don't repurpose one post at a time. Instead:
- Audit your blog's top 10 performing posts (check Google Analytics)
- Spend 2 hours running all 10 through Claude
- Collect 50 pieces of repurposed content in a single day
- Schedule them across platforms over the next 3 months using Buffer or Later

One afternoon of work = three months of social media content.

### Tip 3: A/B Test Repurposed Content

Claude lets you quickly generate *multiple variations* of the same repurposed format. For example:

```
I liked the LinkedIn carousel you created, but let me try another version with a different hook. Can you regenerate the carousel with:
- A more urgent tone
- A different hook (focus on ROI rather than efficiency)
- More controversy/debate to drive engagement
```

Claude will create a variation instantly. Schedule both versions to different LinkedIn accounts or post them a week apart to see which resonates more. This data helps you understand what your audience truly wants.
